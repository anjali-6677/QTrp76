Malaysian Borneo

Wild Jungles, Rare Wildlife, and Indigenous Cultures

About the Region

Malaysian Borneo (Sabah and Sarawak) offers unparalleled natural wonders from Mount Kinabalu to pristine rainforests teeming with wildlife. Home to orangutans, proboscis monkeys, and indigenous tribes, it's an adventure destination with world-class diving, trekking, and cultural experiences.

Key Attractions

  • Kinabalu Park (UNESCO site)
  • Sepilok Orangutan Rehabilitation Centre
  • Sipadan Island (world-class diving)
  • Bako National Park
  • Mulu Caves (world's largest cave chamber)

Travel Tips

  • Best time: March-October (dry season)
  • Vaccinations recommended (consult doctor)
  • Pack leech socks for jungle trekking
  • Respect indigenous cultures and traditions
  • Book dive/accommodation well in advance

Getting There

  • Main airports: Kota Kinabalu (Sabah), Kuching (Sarawak)
  • Domestic flights from KL/Singapore
  • Limited public transport - tours recommended
