Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus

Mumbai's Historic Railway Masterpiece

About the Terminus

Chhatrapati Shivaji Terminus (CST), formerly Victoria Terminus, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of India's most magnificent railway stations. Built in 1887 in the Victorian Gothic Revival style with Indian architectural influences, this iconic landmark serves as the headquarters of the Central Railways. The station handles over 3 million commuters daily and stands as a testament to Mumbai's colonial past and modern vitality.

Architectural Highlights

  • Stunning stone dome, turrets, and pointed arches
  • Beautiful stained glass windows and wood carvings
  • Blend of Victorian Italianate Gothic Revival and traditional Indian architecture
  • Ornate details including gargoyles, sculptures, and bas-reliefs
  • Grand central staircase and ticket hall

Know before you go

  • Open 24 hours for railway passengers
  • Guided heritage walks available on request
  • Photography allowed but respect privacy of commuters
  • Extremely crowded during peak hours (8-11 AM and 5-8 PM)
  • Nearest metro station: CSMT Metro Station

Not allowed

  • Access to restricted operational areas
  • Professional photography without permission
  • Littering or damaging heritage structures
  • Unauthorized commercial activities
