Dudhsagar Waterfalls

The Sea of Milk Falls

About the Waterfalls

Dudhsagar Waterfalls, located on the Goa-Karnataka border, is a four-tiered waterfall with a total height of 310 meters (1017 feet). The name translates to "Sea of Milk" in Hindi, derived from the white foam created by the water as it cascades down the mountain. The falls are surrounded by lush green forests of the Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary.

Experience Highlights

  • Spectacular view of the waterfall from the base
  • Train ride through the falls (during monsoon)
  • Trekking through the Bhagwan Mahavir Wildlife Sanctuary
  • Swimming in the natural pool at the base
  • Photography opportunities with the majestic falls

Know before you go

  • Best time to visit: June to September (monsoon season)
  • Entry restricted during heavy rains for safety
  • Wear comfortable shoes for trekking
  • Carry water and snacks as options are limited
  • No plastic allowed in the sanctuary area

Not included

  • Jeep safari to the falls (additional charge)
  • Guide services
  • Food and beverages
  • Transportation to the starting point
