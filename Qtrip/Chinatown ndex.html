Chinatown, Singapore

Vibrant Cultural District with Rich Heritage

About the Attraction

Singapore's Chinatown is a bustling neighborhood where traditional Chinese culture thrives amidst modern Singapore. With its colorful shophouses, historic temples, authentic eateries and vibrant street markets, it offers an immersive cultural experience and some of the city's best street food.

Key Features

  • Buddha Tooth Relic Temple
  • Chinatown Street Market
  • Maxwell Food Centre (famous hawker center)
  • Thian Hock Keng Temple (oldest Hokkien temple)
  • Club Street (heritage area with bars)

Visitor Information

  • Best time to visit: Evenings for market
  • Market hours: 10am-10pm daily
  • Temple visiting hours: Typically 7am-5pm
  • Try local specialties: chicken rice, bak kut teh
  • Bargain at street markets

Getting There

  • MRT: Chinatown Station (NE4/DT19)
  • Bus services: 2, 12, 33, 54, 143, 147
  • 15-minute walk from Clarke Quay
