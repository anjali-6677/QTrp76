Penang, Malaysia

The Food Capital with UNESCO Heritage Charm

About the Island

Penang blends colonial heritage, street art, and some of Asia's best street food. George Town's UNESCO-listed center features colorful shophouses and temples, while the island offers beautiful beaches and hillside retreats. The perfect mix of culture, history, and culinary delights.

Key Attractions

  • George Town UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Penang Hill (cool retreat with views)
  • Kek Lok Si Temple (largest Buddhist temple)
  • Batu Ferringhi Beach
  • Street art and mural hunting

Travel Tips

  • Best time: November-January
  • Try famous street food: char kway teow, assam laksa
  • Use Grab or public buses for transport
  • Early mornings beat the heat and crowds
  • Carry small change for street vendors

Getting There

  • Penang International Airport
  • Ferries from Butterworth (mainland)
  • 4-5 hour drive from KL
