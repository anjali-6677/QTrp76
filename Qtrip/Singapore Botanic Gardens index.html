Singapore Botanic Gardens

UNESCO World Heritage Tropical Garden Oasis

About the Attraction

Established in 1859, the Singapore Botanic Gardens is the only tropical garden honored as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Spanning 82 hectares, it features lush landscapes, themed gardens, and the famous National Orchid Garden with over 1,000 orchid species and 2,000 hybrids.

Key Features

  • National Orchid Garden (paid section)
  • Swan Lake (oldest ornamental water feature)
  • Rainforest (6-hectare primary rainforest)
  • Jacob Ballas Children's Garden
  • Bandstand and Symphony Lake

Visitor Information

  • Opening hours: 5am-midnight (main garden)
  • Orchid Garden: 8:30am-7pm (last entry 6pm)
  • Orchid Garden admission: SGD 15
  • Free admission to main gardens
  • Guided tours available

Getting There

  • MRT: Botanic Gardens Station (CC19/DT9)
  • Bus services: 7, 75, 77, 105, 106, 123, 174
  • 10-minute walk from Napier Road entrance
