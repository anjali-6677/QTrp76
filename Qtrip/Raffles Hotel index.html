Raffles Hotel, Singapore

Historic Luxury Hotel Where the Singapore Sling Was Invented

About the Attraction

Established in 1887, Raffles Hotel is a colonial-style luxury hotel named after Singapore's founder Sir Stamford Raffles. This national monument is famous for its elegant architecture, impeccable service, and as the birthplace of the Singapore Sling cocktail at the Long Bar.

Key Features

  • Long Bar (home of Singapore Sling)
  • Grand lobby with antique furnishings
  • Shopping arcade with luxury boutiques
  • Raffles Museum
  • Beautiful courtyard gardens

Visitor Information

  • Opening hours: 24/7 (some facilities have limited hours)
  • Singapore Sling price: ~SGD 36
  • Smart casual dress code enforced
  • Free guided heritage tours available
  • Photography allowed in public areas

Getting There

  • MRT: City Hall (EW13/NS25) or Esplanade (CC3)
  • Bus services: 56, 57, 100, 107, 131
  • 10-minute walk from Marina Bay
