Bandra-Worli Sea Link

Mumbai's Engineering Marvel

About the Sea Link

The Bandra-Worli Sea Link is a 5.6 km long, 8-lane cable-stayed bridge that connects Bandra in the Western Suburbs to Worli in South Mumbai. Opened in 2009, this architectural wonder reduces travel time between the two points from 60-90 minutes to just 10 minutes. The bridge stands as a symbol of modern Mumbai, with its sweeping curves and towering cable-stayed portions offering breathtaking views of the Arabian Sea and city skyline.

Experience Highlights

  • Stunning views of Mumbai skyline and Arabian Sea
  • Beautiful illumination at night (7PM-12AM)
  • Photography spots at both ends of the bridge
  • Nearby attractions: Worli Sea Face, Bandra Fort
  • Iconic Mumbai landmark featured in films

Know before you go

  • Toll charges apply for vehicles (₹70-₹140 depending on vehicle type)
  • Pedestrians and two-wheelers not allowed on the bridge
  • Best viewing spots: Worli Sea Face or Bandra Fort
  • Peak traffic hours to avoid: 8-11AM and 5-8PM
  • Monsoon season offers dramatic wave views

Not allowed

  • Stopping or parking on the bridge
  • Walking or cycling across
  • Drone photography without permission
  • Swimming in nearby waters
