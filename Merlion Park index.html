Merlion Park, Singapore

Home of Singapore's Iconic Half-Lion, Half-Fish Symbol

About the Attraction

Merlion Park features Singapore's famous Merlion statue spouting water into Marina Bay. The mythical creature with a lion's head and fish body represents Singapore's origins as a fishing village (Singapura means "Lion City"). The park offers excellent views of Marina Bay Sands and the city skyline.

Key Features

  • 8.6-meter tall Merlion statue
  • Smaller 2-meter Merlion cub statue
  • Panoramic views of Marina Bay
  • Evening light shows from nearby attractions
  • Walking distance to other landmarks

Visitor Information

  • Opening hours: 24/7
  • Free admission
  • Best time to visit: Early morning or evening
  • Can get crowded during peak hours
  • Limited shade - bring sun protection

Getting There

  • MRT: Raffles Place (EW14/NS26)
  • Bus services: 10, 57, 70, 75, 100, 107
  • 10-minute walk from Esplanade MRT
