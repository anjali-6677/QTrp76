Gateway of India Experience

Mumbai's Iconic Historical Monument

About the Gateway

The Gateway of India is an arch-monument built in the early 20th century to commemorate the visit of King George V and Queen Mary to Bombay. Located on the waterfront at Apollo Bunder area in South Mumbai, this basalt structure stands 26 meters tall and blends Hindu and Muslim architectural styles. It served as the ceremonial entrance to India for British viceroys and governors, and today stands as Mumbai's most famous landmark and a popular gathering spot.

Experience Highlights

  • Magnificent views of the Arabian Sea
  • Boat rides to Elephanta Caves available nearby
  • Street photographers and local vendors
  • Proximity to Taj Mahal Palace Hotel
  • Beautiful illumination at night

Know before you go

  • Open 24 hours, best visited early morning or evening
  • Extremely crowded on weekends and holidays
  • Beware of overcharging by photographers and vendors
  • Limited seating available
  • Security checks in place

Not recommended

  • Visiting during peak afternoon heat
  • Carrying large bags or valuables
  • Feeding pigeons (officially prohibited)
  • Swimming in the sea nearby
