Palace of Versailles

Royal Residence of French Kings

About the Palace

Built by Louis XIV in the 17th century, this UNESCO World Heritage site was the political power center of France until the French Revolution. The palace features 2,300 rooms spread over 63,154 m², including the famous Hall of Mirrors, and is surrounded by 800 hectares of formal gardens.

Experience Highlights

  • The Hall of Mirrors (73m gallery with 357 mirrors)
  • King's and Queen's State Apartments
  • Versailles Gardens with fountains and groves
  • Grand Trianon and Marie Antoinette's Estate
  • Musical Fountain Shows and Night Fountains

Know before you go

  • Open Tuesday to Sunday (closed Mondays)
  • Hours: 9AM-6:30PM (gardens close at 8:30PM in summer)
  • Located 20km from Paris (30-45 minute train ride)
  • Free admission for under 18s and EU residents under 26
  • Allow at least 4-5 hours for palace and gardens
